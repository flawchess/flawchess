### Observations

- **Population is mildly weaker at endgames** than non-endgames overall: median per-user diff = -0.031; mean -0.027. This is a real signal, not noise — std of diff (0.118) gives a SE of ~0.004 for the mean. Endgames are technically harder.

- Sanity check: `eg_p50 − non_eg_p50 = 0.5075 − 0.5365 = -0.029` ≈ `diff_p50 = -0.031`. Consistent within rounding ✓.

### Recommendations

| What | Recommended | Currently set | Verdict |

The asymmetry of the gap distribution is the key finding: keeping a symmetric ±0.10 zone implicitly tells most of the population they are "below neutral" at endgames, even though a per-user negative gap of 0.03 is the population median. The fix is either to re-center the band on -0.03 or to widen the negative side to roughly -0.11.

- **Median gap is mildly positive** in all 4 lichess TCs (+10 to +15 Elo) — endgame skill modestly beats actual rating in this sample, consistent with the population having a slightly higher skill in the recovery+parity buckets than implied by their Glicko rating.

- **Clamp saturation is negligible:** 0–1 users out of 1,636 across all combos. The `[0.05, 0.95]` skill clamp is doing its job (preventing blow-up) without distorting the body of the distribution. No need to tune.
- **400-Elo scaling sensitivity:** pooled std_gap ≈ 60–100 Elo, comfortably below the 120–200 "reasonable" range mentioned in the skill spec — i.e. the formula is on the conservative side. Not under-saturating, not over-saturating.

### Observations

- **% of base time:** the typical user range `[p25, p75]` is **substantially narrower than the current ±10pp band** for all TCs except classical:

- **Median bias:** % diff median sits below zero for blitz/rapid/classical (users are slightly *behind* opponents at endgame entry). Bullet is the only TC where median user enters slightly ahead. Not a bug — likely reflects users' increment use vs base burn.

| What | Recommended | Currently set | Verdict |

The clock-pressure gauge is currently calibrated for "typical mid-TC" and over-rewards/over-punishes the extremes (bullet timeout, classical % diff). Per-TC thresholds (or a single tighter ~7pp / ~3pp pair) would make the gauge meaningful again.

Note on SQL approximation: this query takes the clocks at `entry_ply` and `entry_ply + 1`, which misses cases where one of those plies has NULL `clock_seconds` (the backend's stricter scan finds the next non-NULL pair). Bias should be small but is real.

### Observations

- **Pooled score_diff spread across classes: 0.018** (queen +0.005 to minor_piece +0.023) — well within the live ±0.05 band. Pooled gauge zone fits all classes.
- **Pooled conversion spread: 0.094** (minor_piece 0.652 → queen 0.746) — large enough to matter; the conv chart has no neutral zones today, but if added, should be **per-class**, not pooled.
- **Pooled recovery spread: 0.106** (queen 0.258 → minor_piece 0.364) — same conclusion: per-class neutral zones if added.
- **Pattern confirmation:**
  - Queen endgames: easiest to convert (0.75) but hardest to recover (0.26) — losing your queen is usually terminal, having one usually wins.
  - Rook endgames: drawish reputation confirmed — recovery 0.33 is one of the highest among "real" endgames (only beaten by minor_piece 0.36).
  - Minor piece: highest recovery (0.36) — drawing tendency from bishop-pair ↔ knight-pair imbalances and bishops-of-opposite-colors fortresses.
  - Pawn endgames: high conversion (0.69), modest recovery (0.32) — technical but not as dominated by material as queen.
  - Pawnless: very rare (22k games / 779 users — small floor concerns), conversion is queen-like (0.74), recovery is queen-like (0.26).
- **TC slope per class:** every class shows the same monotone "score_diff grows with TC" pattern (bullet < blitz < rapid < classical). Pawn classical score_diff = +0.127 — the only cell that exceeds the live ±0.05 zone substantially (the ±0.30 bullet domain still contains it). Most ELO/TC cells stay inside ±0.05.
- **Pawnless bullet score_diff = -0.039:** the only sizable negative deviation. Likely a population artifact (low-ELO bullet users entering pawnless KBN-like endings without technique). Below the ±0.05 band but inside it.
