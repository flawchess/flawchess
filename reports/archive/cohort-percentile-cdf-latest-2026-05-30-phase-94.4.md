# Cohort Percentile CDF — Sliding-Window Methodology (Phase 94.4)

**Methodology:** Each cell is the 99-breakpoint empirical CDF over the users in the (metric, anchor, TC) cohort, where a user is eligible if their anchor rating is within ±200 Elo of the cell anchor. The cohort takes up to 500 users from the in-window pool (nearest-anchor preferred when capping) and requires at least 100 for the cell to qualify. Ranked in Python after one per-(metric, TC) SQL query joining ``per_user_values × per_user_anchor`` (RESEARCH Pitfall 8). Anchors sweep 800..2600 Elo on a 50-Elo grid (37 anchors per (metric, TC)). A cell is SUPPRESSED when fewer than 100 users fall within ±200 Elo of the anchor (``insufficient_users_in_window``). Cohort size is variable per cell — denser bands yield larger cohorts (tighter CIs) up to the MAX cap; sparser bands sit closer to MIN.

## Relaxation guidance

If coverage in the table above is too sparse for the shipping goal, the operator can choose:

- **Option A — ship current settings**: chip suppresses for users in the affected (metric, anchor, TC) cells; ``interpolate_cohort_percentile`` returns ``None`` → frontend renders nothing.
- **Option B — lower the MIN floor**: lower ``COHORT_MIN_USERS_PER_ANCHOR`` from 100 and rerun the regen. Tradeoff: wider CIs on chips that fall in newly-qualifying cells (~±5pp at n=100, ~±7pp at n=50).
- **Option C — widen the window**: raise ``COHORT_MAX_WINDOW_ELO`` from 200 Elo and rerun the regen. Tradeoff: the chip becomes less peer-relative as the band widens (a 1600-anchored user gets compared against a [1600−W, 1600+W] band).
- **Option D — raise the MAX cap**: raise ``COHORT_MAX_USERS_PER_ANCHOR`` from 500 (or remove the cap) and rerun the regen. Tradeoff: tighter CIs in dense bands, but ``n_users`` becomes more variable across cells.

Inspect the ``n_in_window`` column above to see where MAX is binding (``n_users == MAX < n_in_window``) vs. where MIN is the only floor (``n_users < MAX``).
