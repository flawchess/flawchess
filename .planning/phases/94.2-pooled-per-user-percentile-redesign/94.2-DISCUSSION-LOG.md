## Area 1 — Sparse-cell `(2400, classical)` exclusion

**Context presented:** Current `sparse_exclusion_sql` lives at two layers (cohort selection in `_canonical_selected_users_cte` and per-row WHERE filter in `_per_user_cte_*`). In the pooled-per-user model, "cells" disappear but the games still exist.

**Options presented:**
1. (Recommended) Drop game-row exclusion; keep cohort-selection only (dedup-aware)
2. Keep game-row exclusion: drop games at (≥2400 game-time rating, classical TC) before pooling
3. Drop entirely on both sides

**User chose:** Option 1 (recommended).

**Decision D-1:** Cohort selection skips users only selected via `(2400, classical)` slot (dedup-aware). No game-row WHERE filter in the pooled per-user compute on either side.

## Area 2 — Distinct-user count gate

**Initial question presented:** When + how to gate on distinct-user count per metric (researcher SQL probe / Plan-1 investigation / ship-and-react), with a threshold ladder (N<500 blocker / 500–2000 warning / ≥2000 ship).

**User selected threshold** ≥2000 ship ladder, but pushed back on the framing: "I don't understand what we need a distinct-user count for. It's not users who are sorted into ELO buckets, it's games. Also, we have evals for all games."

**Claude pushed back:** Explained that the 94.1 CDF really does count `(user × elo_bucket × tc_bucket)` cell-rows (`per_user_values` shape) — so dedup-to-user under 94.2 measurably shrinks the CDF point-count. The "we have evals for all games" claim is true for prod but uncertain for the benchmark DB (SEED-006 ingest status). Both concerns resolve in two SQL queries — soft framing (record, don't gate) keeps ROADMAP criterion 8 honest without overweighting.

**User chose:** "Researcher records the numbers, no formal gate." Softened from the original threshold ladder.

**Decision D-2:** Planner's researcher records distinct-user + eval-coverage counts in RESEARCH.md. No formal blocker structure. User reviews numbers when reviewing the plan.

## Area 4 — Tooltip copy: rating-correlation disclosure

**Context presented:** Semantics shift from "canonical-slice career" to "your recent play vs the whole cohort." A 1200 player on `achievable_score_gap` (rating-correlated) will tend to read lower percentile under 94.2 than 94.1. Existing `feedback_popover_copy_minimalism` says "WHAT + sign convention only, no jargon, no caveats."

**Options presented:**
1. (Recommended, matches minimalism) Strict: describe WHAT only, silent on rating correlation
2. Brief honesty: one short line about all-rating calibration
3. Full disclosure: explain rating correlation explicitly

**User chose (custom answer):** Beyond Option 3. Per `reports/benchmarks-gap-metrics-percentile-candidacy.md`, some metrics correlate more or less with ELO and that should be mentioned per metric in tooltips. Also explain: benchmark is all ratings + all TCs; percentile is most recent 1000 games per played TC; UI filters do not affect the percentile.

**Decision D-4:** Tooltip copy per chip discloses four things:
1. Benchmark = all ratings + all TCs
2. Recent-games basis (most recent 1000 per TC, ≤36 months)
3. Filter independence
4. Per-metric rating-correlation framing (none for Endgame Score Gap d=0.19, soft for Achievable/Parity, honest for Conversion d=1.37)

This is a deliberate **exception to copy minimalism for this surface only**. Captured as durable feedback in `feedback_percentile_chip_tooltip_disclosure.md` (linked from MEMORY.md).
