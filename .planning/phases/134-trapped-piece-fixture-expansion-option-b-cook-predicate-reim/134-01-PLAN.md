<objective>
Implement Option B (per-motif oversample cap, D-EXP-02) in the fixture selector, regenerate the committed tagger fixtures so `trapped-piece` reaches ~1000 combined ground-truth rows, and surgically re-measure ONLY the precision floors that the multi-label leakage actually moved.

Purpose: trapped-piece today has only 39 combined ground-truth labels (28 train / 11 test) — too thin to gate or tune honestly. A global `SAMPLES_PER_STRATUM` bump (Option A) was rejected by D-EXP-02 because it reshuffles every motif's fixture and forces re-measuring all 24 floors. Option B isolates the fixture growth to the chosen motif(s). This plan delivers the expanded fixture that Plan 02's detector rewrite iterates against.

Output:
- `scripts/select_tagger_fixtures.py` with a new `--oversample-motifs` CLI arg, an `oversample_map` threaded into `_stratified_sample`, and a per-motif RNG re-seed (Pitfall 1 mitigation) that makes isolation exact for non-co-occurring motifs.
- A new unit test `tests/scripts/test_select_tagger_fixtures.py` exercising `_stratified_sample(oversample_map=...)`.
- Regenerated `fixtures/tagger/detector_fixture_{train,test}.csv` (both committed) with the expanded trapped-piece coverage (+ soft top-ups for en-passant / promotion / under-promotion).
- `tests/scripts/tagger/precision_floors.py` updated ONLY where leakage moved a floor (and the trapped-piece docstring measurement note refreshed to the new fixture size).

AGPL boundary (131 D-10): this plan touches no detector geometry — no cook predicate is reimplemented here. The selector and fixtures carry CC0 lichess data only.
</objective>
