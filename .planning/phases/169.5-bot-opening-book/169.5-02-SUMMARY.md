## Decisions Made
- The floor check reads `rawPolicy` directly and sits textually *before* the `weighting(...)` call, with a prominent comment at the site explaining why (single-candidate renormalized share is always 1.0, and a persona weighting may reshape the distribution arbitrarily)
- `maiaPolicyWeighting` performs no renormalization — documented in place so nobody later "fixes" it by dividing by the sum (that would rescale every weight by the same constant and change nothing, at the cost of a redundant float step)
- Degenerate-policy handling (NaN/Infinity/all-zero) is *not* re-implemented here: `botSampling`'s `weightedPick` already returns `null` on a non-finite or non-positive total (D-13), and that `null` propagates untouched as another leave-book signal (T-169.5-03 mitigation)
