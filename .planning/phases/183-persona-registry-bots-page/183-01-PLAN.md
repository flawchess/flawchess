<objective>
Build the Phase 183 persona data foundation: a single typed 24-slot registry, the placeholder-avatar map, the last-used color/TC persistence helper, the committed avatar-generation prompts, and the per-style accent colors — the keystone every downstream plan consumes.

Purpose: PERS-03 demands one typed registry mapping each of 24 persona slots (4 styles × 6 rungs) to its complete pinned config, with the rung→preset table dictated by the measured ranges. AVAT-02 needs authored names + bios; AVAT-01 ships placeholder avatars (D-16/D-18) plus committed prompts (D-15) for the future real-art PR.
Output: `personaRegistry.ts` (PersonaId/Rung/Persona types, PERSONA_REGISTRY, RUNG_BLEND, STYLE_SECTION_ORDER, personaForId lookup), `personaAvatars.ts`, `botPersonaSetupSettings.ts`, `personaAvatarPrompts.md`, per-style accent constants in `theme.ts`, and shape-assertion tests.

Unresolved-edge assumptions surfaced (spec-less probe fallback): (A1) `botElo === rung` for all 24 personas is the confirmed plan decision for this pre-calibration phase — Phase 184 revisits it; documented explicitly in the registry header so Phase 184 has an auditable baseline. The 1600-rung Light-vs-Deep split per persona is Claude's discretion informed by `reports/data/bot-strength-lookup.json` measured ranges (Light 1500–1600, Deep 1600–1800) — no even split is required.
</objective>

<assumption_delta_decision>
Noun now primary: the **persona** (a curated, named identity that pins a complete `BotGameSettings`) generalizes the raw `(ELO, preset, style)` knob layer that Phase 182/171 exposed.
Decision: **add-alongside** (accepted debt). Personas become the default Bots-page surface; the raw-knob SetupScreen (Custom mode) stays an untouched escape hatch. CONTEXT.md D-01 and PERS-04 lock this — the registry builds a `BotGameSettings` with an optional `personaId`, it does NOT replace or subsume the Custom path.
Rationale: promoting personas to the ONLY surface would delete PERS-04's power-user contract; add-alongside preserves both audiences at the cost of two parallel start entries funneled through one `handleStart`.
What would force a later promote: if Custom-mode usage drops to near-zero in analytics, or if a future requirement needs persona identity on every bot game (removing the `undefined` personaId branch), collapse Custom into a "fully-custom persona" and promote.
Invariant test encoding the generalized intent: `personaRegistry.test.ts` asserts every one of the 24 persona slots resolves to a complete, valid `BotGameSettings` (botElo in ladder, blend in {HUMAN,LIGHT,DEEP}, style === a BOT_STYLE_BUNDLES singleton) — i.e. every persona slot is a total function to a playable opponent.
</assumption_delta_decision>
