## The recurring off-by-one — how it was nailed

The violet arrow's index was derived empirically, not assumed. `bestMoveByPly.get(j)` =
engine best move from `mainLine[j-1]` (the blue following-best uses `get(k+1)` = best from
the displayed `mainLine[k]`). The should-have-played move is the best from the pre-flaw
decision position `mainLine[k-1]` = `get(k)`. The `useGameOverlay.test.ts` parity test uses
a per-ply-distinct `best_move` fixture so any off-by-one (`get(k+1)` or `get(k-1)`) is
caught: it asserts the violet arrow = decision-position best (= the move FlawCard's blue
arrow shows for the same flaw) and that it is distinct from the blue following-best, with
no depth label on the blue arrow. Plus no-violet-arrow guards for allowed-only and clean
plies.
