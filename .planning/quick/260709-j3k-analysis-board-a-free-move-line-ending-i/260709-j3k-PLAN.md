# Quick Task 260709-j3k: Checkmate on a free move line reads as a blunder

## Problem

On the `/analysis` board, playing a free (sideline / free-play) move that delivers
checkmate marks the mating move as a **blunder** and snaps the Stockfish eval bar
back to the midpoint (~50%, "M0").

**Root cause.** A checkmated position reports an ambiguous engine eval: the live
Stockfish worker returns `mate 0` (or no score) for the terminal position. Two
consumers then misread it:

1. `EvalBar.computeWhiteFraction` treats `evalMate === 0` as a mate-in-0 terminal
   and renders the **0.5 midpoint** (`src/components/analysis/EvalBar.tsx:32-36`).
2. `evalToExpectedScore` treats `evalMate === 0` as "no mate" and falls through to
   a null `evalCp` â†’ expected score **0.5** (`src/lib/liveFlaw.ts:60-66`). The live
   classifier (`useLiveMoveFlaw`) then sees the mover's expected score drop from a
   winning ~1.0 (parent) to 0.5 (child) â†’ drop â‰Ą 0.15 â†’ **blunder**.

Both bugs share one fix: derive the terminal eval **deterministically from the
rules** (chess.js) instead of trusting the engine's ambiguous terminal output.

## Fix

Add a pure helper `terminalPositionEval(fen)` and wire it into the two consumers on
the analysis board. A checkmate = the side to move is the loser, so the eval is a
decisive white-POV mate for the other side; a draw/stalemate is dead-equal (cp 0).
