<objective>
Fix misleading prose in the Maia and FlawChess `/analysis` cards so it reflects the addressed player's objective STANDING (winning/losing), not just move DIFFICULTY, and so inline evals are shown from the addressed player's frame instead of raw white-POV.

Two braided bugs:
1. Maia card tier logic (`computePositionVerdict`, badMass-based) measures difficulty of play, not standing. In a dead-lost position badMass ≈ 0, so it emits the `safe` tier → "this position is safe for you" while the player is being mated.
2. Both cards render evals white-POV and never re-sign to the addressed player, so "safe for you — Qxc1 (-M4)" shows a number from the wrong frame.

Approach is LOCKED (Option B from a `/gsd-explore` session): a "{standing clause} — {difficulty clause}" Maia sentence plus a shared player-POV eval formatter. Do NOT redesign.

Purpose: the cards must tell the truth about whether the player is winning or losing.
Output: shared player-POV formatter, standing-band classifier, rewritten Maia prose, minor FlawChess corrections, full test coverage.
</objective>
