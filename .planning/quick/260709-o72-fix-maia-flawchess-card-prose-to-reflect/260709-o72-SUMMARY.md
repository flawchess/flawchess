# Quick 260709-o72: Fix Maia/FlawChess Card Prose to Reflect Standing, Not Just Difficulty Summary

**Rewrote the Maia and FlawChess `/analysis` card prose to lead with the addressed player's objective standing (winning/losing/mate) via a new StandingBand classifier and a shared player-POV eval formatter, replacing a badMass-only "safe for you" sentence that could read as safe while the player was actually being mated.**

## Accomplishments

- `formatPlayerPovEval` — single shared formatter that re-signs a white-POV cp/mate eval to the mover's frame, delegating string formatting to `positionVerdict`'s existing `formatVerdictEval` (DRY)
- `positionVerdict.ts` gained `StandingBand`, `classifyStanding`, and an extended `computePositionVerdict(..., mover)` returning `standing`/`standingEvalCp`/`standingEvalMate`/`bestMove` — the objective standing is now computed independently of the difficulty (badMass) tier
- Maia card's `renderVerdictSentence` rewritten to "{standing clause} — {difficulty clause}" with a deterministic collapse rule: `mate-against` always collapses to a "longest resistance" sentence; `{mate-for-you, winning, losing} + safe` collapses to a single best-move clause; `level` never collapses (its own knife-edge framing); everything else keeps both clauses
- FlawChess card's sentence eval chips (`sfEvalText`/`fcEvalText`/aligned `evalText`) switched from `formatScore` (white-POV) to `formatPlayerPovEval(..., mover)`; sharp-tier copy changed from "the safer" to "the more reliable"
- `mover: MoverColor` threaded through `MaiaMoveQualityBar` → `MaiaHumanPanel`, wired from `Analysis.tsx` (`sideToMoveFromFen(position)`) at both the desktop and mobile `MaiaHumanPanel` call sites
