<objective>
Fix the tactic-depth orientation asymmetry (user's "Option A") and relabel the
depth presets Beginner/Intermediate/Advanced → Low/Medium/High.

The DB columns `missed_tactic_depth` (player's own best line, anchored on the
flaw_ply PV) and `allowed_tactic_depth` (opponent's refutation, flaw_ply+1 PV)
both store the raw 0-based detector loop index within their OWN PV. On the
FlawCard / LibraryGameCard miniboards BOTH depth badges are anchored on the SAME
pre-flaw board (flaw.fen). Because the opponent's refutation line begins one ply
later, an allowed tactic at raw depth d is actually one ply deeper from the
shared decision board than a missed tactic at the same raw d.

Option A (LOCKED — do NOT relitigate): keep the DB columns as the raw detector
index. NO migration, NO backfill, NO change to the detector or the
flaws_service write path. Apply a +1 "decision-anchored" offset for the ALLOWED
orientation at READ TIME ONLY, in exactly two places: the backend depth filter
(library_repository) and the frontend display (tacticDepth.ts), plus the two
miniboard label sites that consume it.

Purpose: make allowed and missed tactic depths directly comparable on a single
decision-anchored difficulty scale, both in the filter and on screen; modernize
the preset labels to neutral Low/Medium/High.
Output: orientation-aware display + filter offset, renamed presets, updated tests.
</objective>
