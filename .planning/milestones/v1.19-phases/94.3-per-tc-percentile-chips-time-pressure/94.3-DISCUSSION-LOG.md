## Area 2 — Net Flag Rate chip framing

**Question 1:** Net Flag Rate is lower-is-better. How should the chip surface that inversion?

**Options:**
- Invert at storage — store `100 - raw_percentile` for net_flag_rate metrics; chip stays direction-agnostic.
- Direction prop on chip — store raw percentile uniformly; add `direction: 'higher_is_better' | 'lower_is_better'` prop; chip flips rendering.
- Both: invert at storage + popover prose discloses direction.

**Selected:** Direction prop on chip.

**Locked in CONTEXT.md as:** D-2 (raw percentile stored uniformly; direction prop flips text formatter + color bands + flame trigger).

**Question 2:** Should the popover body explicitly call out "Lower is better", or rely on the chip's color + text alone?

**Options:**
- Explicit "Lower is better" line — prepended to the 4-bullet body for net_flag_rate flavors only.
- No special line — trust chip color + text + flames to communicate direction.

**Selected:** Explicit "Lower is better" line.

**Locked in CONTEXT.md as:** D-3 (deliberate addition to the 4-bullet contract for the four `net_flag_rate_{tc}` flavors only).

## Area 3 — Clock Gap chip semantics

**Question:** Clock Gap is signed. What does "Top X%" mean on this chip?

**Options:**
- Furthest positive = best — top 5% = user has the most clock-advantage at endgame entry.
- Closest to zero = best — top 5% = most balanced clock management (folded distribution).
- Smallest deficit, advantage capped — top 5% = least clock disadvantage; positive gap clamped.

**Selected:** Furthest positive = best.

**Locked in CONTEXT.md as:** D-4 (skill-aligned framing; raw percentile maps directly; chronic deficit honestly reads "Top 80%+" / red band).
