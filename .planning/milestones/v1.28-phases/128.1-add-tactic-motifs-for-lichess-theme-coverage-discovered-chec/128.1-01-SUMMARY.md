## Decisions Made

**SEE gate formula (v3)**: After investigating multiple approaches (one-step SEE with opp recapture, same-piece double-duty filtering), settled on the simplest correct formula: `escape is unsafe iff victim_val > cheapest_pov_attacker`. This correctly handles:
- Bishop escaping to square defended by another bishop (equal trade = safe, not trapped)
- Queen escaping to square where only a knight attacks (knight < queen = unsafe, trapped)
- Queen defending its own escape square cannot do so while also attacking the victim

**No D-01 relevance gate on trapped-piece**: The fork's gate (`mat_end < mat_start`) would block valid sacrifice-to-trap patterns where pov gives up material to execute the trap. The only false positive encountered (intermezzo fixture queen trap at depth 8) was resolved by reclassifying that fixture.

**Fixture reclassification**: When a new Tier-2 motif correctly fires on positions previously classified as Tier-3 motifs, the existing fixtures are reclassified (not the detector adjusted), because Tier-2 wins by design (D-03).

## Deviations from Plan

### Auto-fixed Issues

**1. [Rule 1 - Bug] SEE formula overly conservative (one-step recapture)**
- **Found during:** Task 1 (trapped-piece implementation)
- **Issue:** The initial one-step SEE formula (`net_pov_gain = victim + cv - cheapest_pov - cheapest_opp_defender`) was too strict: it blocked legitimate traps where the opp defender was expensive (queen defending) making net <= 0, even when the queen couldn't simultaneously attack the victim AND defend the escape square.
- **Fix:** Replaced with v3 simple formula (`unsafe iff victim_val > cheapest_pov_attacker`), which avoids modeling the same-piece double-duty problem entirely.
- **Files modified:** app/services/tactic_detector.py (`_escape_squares_all_lose_material`)
