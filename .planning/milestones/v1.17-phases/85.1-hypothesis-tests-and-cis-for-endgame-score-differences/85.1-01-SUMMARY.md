## Decisions Made
- **Inlined trinomial variance formula rather than factoring it out.** The formula `max(0.0, (w + 0.25 * d) / n - score ** 2)` appears in `compute_confidence_bucket` (single cohort) and now in `compute_score_difference_test` (per-cohort, twice). Extracting a `_trinomial_variance(w, d, n)` helper was considered but rejected: two call sites is below the "rule of three" threshold, and the inline form keeps the SE_diff derivation readable as one self-contained block. Revisit if Plan 02+ adds a third call site.
- **Used `collections.abc.Sequence` over `typing.Sequence`.** The plan said to "widen the existing typing import" but the project hasn't standardized; `collections.abc.Sequence` is the non-deprecated runtime-friendly import path (per CPython 3.9+ guidance) and behaves identically as a type annotation under PEP 585. Plan-level intent (covariant input parameter per CLAUDE.md) is preserved.
- **n=1 short-circuit in `compute_paired_difference_test` returns the mean unconditionally.** Bessel correction divides by `n-1`; at `n=1` that is zero. The helper returns `(mean_d, None, None, None)` rather than `(0.0, None, None, None)` — the mean is still well-defined and may be useful to callers for display ("one observation, mean = 0.1, p/CI undefined").
