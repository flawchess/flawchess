#### Findings (for downstream phases)

- **Bullet** is uniformly low-signal (4–11%) regardless of player rating. ~4
  eval positions per bullet game.
- **2400-rated non-bullet** is exceptionally high signal (76–86%). Strong
  players use Lichess analysis at very high rates.
- **2000+ rapid/classical** is in the 54–60% range — usable for fine-grained
  endgame analytics.
- **Classical is the absolute-volume constraint**, not bullet: smoke shows
  ~482 games/user classical vs ~8,708 bullet. Combined with selection-scan
  candidate ceilings (800/classical=78, 2400/classical=24) the classical
  stripe will always be the smallest by raw eval-position count.

#### Optimization decision

We considered (a) asymmetric per-TC user counts, (b) skipping bullet, (c)
filter-at-import on eval presence, (d) higher K threshold, (e) boosting
classical only. None are justified at Phase 69 scope:

- Skipping bullet violates Success Criterion 3 (all 20 cells populated).
- Higher K biases selection toward analytics-heavy players, contaminating
  Phase 73 baselines.
- Filter-at-import breaks full-population analytics in Phase 73.
- Boosting classical hits the candidate ceilings.

Decision: keep `--per-cell 30` symmetric. Even bullet yields ~1M eval positions
per cell at this volume — sufficient for stratified endgame analytics. Phase 70
/ 73 should weight cells by their eval-position count when comparing across
TCs rather than treating cells as equal.
