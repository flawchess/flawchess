<objective>
The pure book module: candidate filter â†’ exit-rule check â†’ policy-weighted sample. No React, no providers, no I/O â€” a synchronous function of (history, legal moves, prefix set, raw policy, rng).

Two things in here are easy to get subtly, silently wrong, and both are covered by explicit tests:

1. **The floor must be checked on the RAW policy.** If you check it on the weighting function's output (the renormalized book subset), then a position with exactly one book candidate always has a renormalized share of 1.0 â€” the floor can never fire, precisely in the single-candidate case it exists to catch. The code must compute `clearsFloor` from `rawPolicy` BEFORE the weighting function is ever called.

2. **"Openings vary" is not proof the weighting works.** A sampler that silently degrades to uniform-over-book-candidates also produces multiple distinct first moves over many draws. The variety test must assert empirical frequency TRACKS the stubbed policy weights, or it is worthless.

Purpose: the selection + exit-rule half of SC2/SC3/SC4.
Output: `frontend/src/lib/engine/openingBook.ts` + its test.
</objective>
