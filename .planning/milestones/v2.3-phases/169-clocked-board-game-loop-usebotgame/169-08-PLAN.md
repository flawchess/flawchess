<objective>
Replace the bot's dishonest clock math with an honest one, and build the
deadline-cut search wrapper the bot needs to survive on that honest clock.

Two pure, React-free modules, both directly unit-testable:

1. `chessClock.ts` — DELETE the synthetic-debit + never-flag machinery
   (D-15: it converges to a ~6s equilibrium at 5+3 and is the root cause of the
   SC1 verification failure), and ADD `computeThinkDeadlineMs` (D-16), the
   engine-style per-move think deadline derived from the bot's remaining clock.
2. `deadlineSearch.ts` (net-new) — `createDeadlineSearch`, a `SearchRunner`
   wrapper that cuts an in-flight search at the deadline and returns its
   best-so-far snapshot (D-16/D-17), gated by a minimum-node floor (D-18).

Purpose: The bot is now allowed to lose on time (169 D-15, amended ROADMAP SC1).
An honest clock with a fixed search budget is degenerate — at 5+3 with a ~5.4s
median search and a 3s increment the bot bleeds ~2.4s/move and any user wins by
shuffling. The deadline is what makes an honest clock survivable: the bot speeds
up when low, and flags only when it genuinely cannot move in time.

Output: honest `chessClock.ts` + `computeThinkDeadlineMs`; `deadlineSearch.ts`
with behavioral tests for the cut, the node floor, and cancel propagation.
</objective>
