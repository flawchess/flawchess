<task type="checkpoint:human-verify" gate="blocking">
  <name>Task 1: Human UAT of the full clocked bot game on /bots</name>
  <action>Pause for human verification: the developer runs the nine-point browser checklist below on the /bots route and either approves or returns a punch list. No code is written in this task — it is a blocking acceptance gate before /gsd-verify-work.</action>
  <what-built>
  A full clocked bot game on the /bots route: dual Fischer clocks, 168.5 bot
  pacing (fixed budget + reveal delay + never-flag synthetic debit), all end
  conditions incl. flag-on-time, resign confirm + draw offer/accept/decline,
  vendored move/capture/check/game-end + low-time + draw-declined sounds with a
  mute toggle, a dismissible result dialog + persistent strip, view-only move
  scroll-back, and an Analyze deep-link — behind a hardcoded-settings stub.
  </what-built>
  <how-to-verify>
  Start the dev server (`cd frontend && npm run dev`; backend + dev DB per
  CLAUDE.md are NOT required — the game is fully client-side) and open
  http://localhost:5173/bots (adjust port if Vite reports another).

  1. PLAY-03: Make a move by drag AND by click-to-click; confirm illegal/
     off-turn moves snap back and the Fischer increment adds to your clock after
     each move. Confirm you cannot move while the bot is thinking.
  2. PLAY-05/D-05/D-06: Watch a contested-position bot reply — the bot clock
     must tick DOWN in real time (never frozen), a pulsing "thinking" dot shows,
     and on a fast move a reveal delay floors it (not instant). The bot clock
     must never hit 0:00.
  3. PLAY-04: During the bot's think, switch to another tab for ~30s, come back
     — neither clock should have been unfairly drained for the hidden interval.
  4. D-07: Let your clock drop under ~10s — digits turn red and show tenths
     (0:09.4); the low-time warning sound fires ONCE at the crossing.
  5. PLAY-06: Reach at least one end condition (easiest: let your clock flag, or
     play a quick checkmate/stalemate); confirm the result dialog shows the
     correct outcome+reason.
  6. PLAY-07/D-04: Click Resign — confirm the two-step dialog; cancel, then
     confirm it. Start a new game, click Offer draw — confirm it's throttle-
     disabled shortly after a decline (tooltip), and that a decline plays the
     blip sound. Confirm the bot never offers a draw or resigns.
  7. PLAY-08/D-08/Pitfall-4: Toggle Mute on/off (persists across reload).
     Judge the vendored sfx sounds' tonal fit (RESEARCH Open Question 1 — if
     they feel off-brand, note which of piano/futuristic/nes to try instead).
     If possible, verify on a real iOS device that the bot's FIRST move sound
     fires after your first tap (autoplay unlock).
  8. PLAY-09/D-11/D-12: On game end, dismiss the dialog (final position shows,
     strip persists), click "Analyze this game" (lands on /analysis with your
     moves), and "New game" (restarts).
  9. D-13: Click an earlier move / use arrow keys — the board goes view-only
     with a visible "Return to live position" affordance; new moves only apply
     at the live position.
  </how-to-verify>
  <resume-signal>Type "approved" if all checks pass, or list the failing items (with which of piano/futuristic/nes to try if the sfx sounds feel off) to drive a gap-closure pass.</resume-signal>
</task>
