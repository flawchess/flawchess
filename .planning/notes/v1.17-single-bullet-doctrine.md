# Single-bullet doctrine for v1.17 Conv/Parity/Recov/Skill cards

## Decision

Drop the cohort/p50 bullet from Section 2 (Conv/Parity/Recov/Skill) and Section 3 (per-type breakdown). Each card carries a **single peer bullet** (`You − Opp` vs 0) using the mirror-bucket opponent baseline already on `/api/endgames/overview`.

This replaces the original "two-bullet doctrine (cohort + peer)" framing in the v1.17 roadmap and REQUIREMENTS.md draft.

## Why

Three candidate signals were weighed during exploration:

| Signal | Reference | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|--------|-----------|-----------|------------|
| Cohort vs global p50 | Median of all benchmark players (across rating tiers) | Stable, filter-independent | Rating-tier confound — a 1200 is compared to a pool that includes 2400s. Roughly redundant with what ELO already tells the user. |
| Peer vs Opp (`You − Opp` vs 0) | Opponent's mirror-bucket rate (same rating × TC) | Rating-tier conditioned by construction. Filter-responsive. Self-calibrating. Already on the API. | Doesn't tell you whether the gap itself is typical at your level. |
| Tier-gap baseline (your gap vs distribution of per-player gaps at your tier) | p50 of `(player − their opponents)` across players in your rating × TC bucket | Strongest signal. Removes both the tier confound *and* the "maybe opponents are weak" trivialization. | Requires new benchmark precomputation (per-player gap distributions). Out of scope for v1.17. |

The cohort/p50 bullet has a known rating-tier confound (the v1.17 draft even called for popover copy to disclose it). For users this is effectively a noisier restatement of their ELO. The peer bullet does not have this weakness: the mirror bucket matches by rating × TC, so the comparison group is "players at my level facing me."

The tier-gap baseline is the strongest of the three, but the benchmark pipeline change is significant — new per-bucket distributions of per-player gaps, new aggregation, min-pool gating, sig-test methodology decision. Deferred to a future milestone via seed.

Net effect: the peer frame on its own is the cleanest honest signal we can ship in v1.17, and the cohort frame's added value is small enough that the second bullet costs more in cognitive load than it returns.

## Skill card — Option B (derived peer baseline)

Skill is a composite metric, so there is no direct mirror-bucket opponent rate. Two passable options were on the table:

- **A:** drop the bullet on Skill, accept the visual asymmetry (gauge + percent only).
- **B:** derive `Opp Skill` from the existing mirror-bucket components (`opp_conv`, `opp_recov` already on the API) using the same composite formula as `Your Skill`, then show a peer bullet `Your Skill − Opp Skill` vs 0.

We chose **B**: it keeps the 4-card layout symmetric and uses data that's already exposed. The sig-test methodology for the composite is a plan-phase decision (Wald-z directly on the derived difference, propagation from component CIs, or computation on raw underlying outcomes). See SEC2-08.

## Affected requirements

Rewritten in place: SEC2-02, SEC2-03, SEC2-04, SEC2-06, SEC2-08, SEC2-09, SEC3-02, SEC3-05, SEC1-05.
Dropped: SEC2-05 (cohort sig test), SEC3-03 (per-type p50), DATA-01 (per-type p50 codegen).
v1 requirement count: 30 → 27.

## Out of scope (seed)

The tier-gap baseline signal is not abandoned, just deferred. Trigger to revisit: benchmark precomputation pipeline rework, or v1.17 user feedback indicating users want a "is my edge actually big *for my level*" framing.
